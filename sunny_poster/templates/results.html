1) 3D MCLE Pipeline

Representative results in one slice of left ventricle (LV):

(a) Steady state (SS) image and the T1* map along with MR signal recovery curves

(b) Fuzzy clustering scatter plot for all pixels within LV

(c) Corresponding tissue map with 3 clustered zones: healthy, BZ and dense scar

3D MR-derived LV model with three tissue zones (healthy, BZ and scar) obtained from the T1*-SS parametric maps

2) 3D LGE Pipeline

A comparison of scar (green) and BZ (yellow) tissue characterization using 3 to 6 STD’s in SI.

Note that tissue characterization was highly dependent on which sample regions were identified as scar and healthy. LGE imaging provides lower contrast between blood and scar tissue, due to their similar SI’s.

Visual comparison between:

(a) 3D LV model from LGE segmentation

(b) EAM obtained from LV-endo, with voltage amplitude in ECG waveforms used to identify healthy, BZ, and scar (scar <0.5mV, 0.51.5mV)

3) Data Analysis

Linear fittings and correlations between infarct size in the LV and VTCL showing:

a) A strong correlation between MR-derived infarct size and VTCL

b) A weak correlation coefficient between EAM-defined infarct areas and VTCL. Notably, the MRI-derived infarct size yields a better correlation with VTCL than EAM-derived infarcts.

Fusion of EAM’s with the MCLE segmentation showed good correspondence between low voltage areas and MCLE-derived infarct (Vurtigo).
